Architectural Nomenclature

OpenTendril models autonomous execution through a strict botanical metaphor. Click the events below to visualize the lifecycle of a Sprout.

Boot

1. initiate()

The main orchestrator boots up. The core structural stem grows and establishes the environment, preparing Branches for Sprouts.

Idle

2. circumnutate()

A Sprout is in queue or polling for a trigger. In nature, a tendril physically swings in circles through the air waiting for contact.

Spawn

3. sprout() / emerge()

A task is received. A short-lived, ephemeral Sprout rapidly breaks through the surface to handle the specific task.

Execute

4. extend() & elongation()

The Sprout executes the ReAct loop (elongation), reaching out across the network to hook into an API, database, or tool.

Resolution A

5a. anchor() / latch()

For pinned Sprouts (like an active Model Context Protocol connection), the tendril wraps securely around the target and becomes a permanent structural tether.

Resolution B

5b. senesce() / drop()

For ephemeral run-once Sprouts, the task completes. The tendril drops its context, dries up, and terminates cleanly to free memory.
